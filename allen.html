Who Snitched On LEIGH?

By Tom Voigt Founder, Zodiackiller.com tomvoigt@zodiackiller.com

In early October 1969 — a couple of years before Arthur Leigh Allen's former best friend, Don Cheney, came forward to police with incriminating statements indicating Allen was the Zodiac killer — someone had already tipped off the Vallejo Police Department that Allen was worth looking at.

Who was that first accuser, and why did they suspect Arthur Leigh Allen was the Zodiac killer?

Those questions bring us to the most recent piece of Arthur Leigh Allen misinformation circling the Internet drain: That Allen had originally faced police scrutiny because the Zodiac killer had been proven to drive a small car — the same type Allen drove.

The claim — initially posted at Reddit and then regurgitated elsewhere — is nonsense. However, that hasn't stopped it from gaining traction among the misinformed. So, why do I care what misinformed individuals share on Reddit?

The silly story goes something like this: Following the Zodiac killer's September 1969 attack in its jurisdiction, the Napa County Sheriff's Department measured the tire impressions left by the Zodiac killer at the crime scene. Those impressions were consistent with a small car. That bit of evidence eventually led to Arthur Leigh Allen being scrutinized as a suspect, because Allen drove a small car.

Reminder: In the above paragraph, the only true detail is the part where the police measured the Zodiac killer's tire impressions.

KNOWLEDGE BOMB: While the Napa investigators did measure the tire impressions left by the Zodiac killer, it was never concluded the impressions were made by a small car. In fact, quickly thereafter the focus was on finding a car that would match impressions consistent with a large vehicle — specifically, a 1966 Corvair or similar. Additionally, the Napa tire impressions played no role in Arthur Leigh Allen being identified as a suspect. None. It never happened.

Was Bob Luce the original tipster who gave the Vallejo police Allen's name back in October 1969, as being the possible Zodiac killer? Impossible to say, as nobody knows for sure who the tipster was. However, unlike the Reddit nonsense about tires and small cars, the Luce theory is at least plausible and based on actual, documented facts from the case file.
